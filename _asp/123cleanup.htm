One of our members had the original idea for the Institute of Database Developers to build a database and web site to support national "Clean Up" activities. We believe that by giving permanent credit to the volunteers and sponsors via the Internet, we will add new enthusiasm for a clean America. We hope to involve the White House in support of this non-controversial project. Our contacts with the Sierra Club and other organizations indicate solid support outside of government, which should increase our chances with the new administration.

CONCEPT: Groups all over America do volunteer work all the time by cleaning up areas that need it. For example the Sierra Club has on-going projects that clean up rivers. Currently the work is done; everyone benefits, but no one gets lasting credit. For the most part, it is a silent deed.

But when you give credit by acknowledging the volunteers personally and permanently, a new dynamic is added. You will see more volunteers for many new reasons. You will see more sponsors. Public contests will take place about who does the best job. Governors, schools and civic clubs will challenge for bragging rights.

The best way to do this is through a permanent and accessible listing of clean up activities on the Internet. The proposed project will be called "123 Clean Up". The 123 means 3 Steps:

  1. Organize a clean up activity.
  2. Do the clean up.
  3. Log on to 123cleanup.org and report your accomplishments.

THE WEB SITE PLAN: The IODD has the greatest skill set of any group in the country for this kind of project. We focus on this kind of task every day. Our experience with very large data transfers and live updating by individuals make this a routine project. We have the resources to provide servers and connectivity for the initial phases, therefore no cash is required to kick this web site off.

After a clean up effort is completed in some area of the country, a person will logon to 123CleanUp.org. The following items will be asked for:

  • The person's name and other contact information
  • The sponsor of the clean up effort and contact info, if any
  • The location cleaned with City, County, State etc.
  • The estimated number of equivalent green yard trash bags collected
  • The dates of the effort
  • A description with anecdotes etc.
  • The names and ages of all volunteers

With this information coming in from all over the country we can produce great statistics, like:

  • Which City or County or State collected the most by week by month and by year. (Suppose the White House would give a monthly or annual award the winners.)
  • A volunteer "Wall of Fame" with the name of every volunteer and their ages when they participated. (Imagine school children looking their names up on the Internet when they are 10, and doing it again when they are 30 to show their children.)
  • Charts would show the increasing numbers of tons of trash removed from our environment by volunteers sweat, not government dollars.

THE PROMOTION PLAN: As always, success of a project like this requires solid promotion and a good sponsor. Members Bruce Troutman and Bruce Parker have some personal and professional contacts that should open some doors to further this goal. And .. with a little imagination, you might see the whole world participating.
